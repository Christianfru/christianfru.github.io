PERSONAL PROFILE

Academic and Career journey

What I am today is the outcome of a several encounters in my personal and professional life which contributed in shaping my academic and career choices. Seeing people suffering from lack of access to safe drinking water as well as malnutrition has always been very challenging and disheartening for me.

My exposure to people in such vulnerable situations was heightened during the period when I served as an Operations Manager with FoodCo, a cereal manufacturing company in Bamenda, Cameroon.

Chevening Scholar, 2018

In the year 2018, When I applied and got selected for the prestigious Chevening Scholarship. I stayed true to my cause and opted to go in for an MSc in WASH at the University of Leeds in the UK. During my post-graduate study program, I network with a wide variety of industry experts, tapping into their experience in the WASH sector. After successfully obtaining an MSc in Water Sanitation and Health Engineering, I returned to Cameroon in 2020. Instead of moving to my hometown, Bamenda, I stayed in the nation’s capital, Yaoundé, where my wife and kids now stayed as Internally Displaced Persons.

Founding RDWB, 2020

Without losing any steam, I set out to work on founding Research and Development Without Borders (RDWB), an organisation that has come to serve as Term of Reference in Cameroon’s not for profit non-governmental sector. For over 4 years, I, together with the team at RDWB have put in a lot of work and energy into several community development projects, which range from improved sources of drinking water, to sustainable agricultural practices and finally, to youth empowerment. at the time when RDWB was becoming firmly rooted in the soil, Cameroon, just like the rest of the world was hit by the Corona Virus Pandemic and we immediately launched the back-to-school cleaning campaign.
